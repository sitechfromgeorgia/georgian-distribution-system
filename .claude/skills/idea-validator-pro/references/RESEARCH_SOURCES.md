# Research Sources Reference

## Overview

This guide shows you where to find the best data for validating product ideas. Each source is rated for reliability, speed, and depth.

---

## Primary Research Sources

### 1. Reddit

**Best For:** Finding real user pain points and complaints
**Reliability:** ★★★★☆ (High - authentic user voices)
**Speed:** ★★★★★ (Very Fast - search is instant)

#### Key Subreddits:
- **r/SaaS** - SaaS discussions, launches, problems
- **r/startups** - Startup ideas and challenges
- **r/Entrepreneur** - Business problems and solutions
- **r/smallbusiness** - SMB pain points
- **r/freelance** - Freelancer workflows and tools
- **r/productivity** - Productivity tool discussions
- **r/webdev** - Developer tools and workflows
- **r/marketing** - Marketing tool discussions

#### How to Research:
1. Search: `site:reddit.com [your problem] sucks`
2. Search: `site:reddit.com alternatives to [competitor]`
3. Search: `site:reddit.com [problem] solutions`
4. Look at comment threads, not just posts
5. Check post timestamps (recent = more relevant)

#### What to Look For:
- Recurring complaints about existing tools
- Workarounds people have created
- "I wish there was a tool that..." statements
- Price complaints (signals WTP exists)
- Feature requests that go unaddressed

**Example Search:**
```
site:reddit.com "invoice software" freelance sucks
```

### 3. Indie Hackers

**Best For:** Solo founder perspectives and revenue data
**Reliability:** ★★★★★ (Excellent - verified revenue)
**Speed:** ★★★☆☆ (Moderate - requires browsing)

#### Where to Look:
- **Milestones:** Real revenue numbers from founders
- **Product Pages:** Detailed product descriptions
- **Interviews:** Deep-dive founder stories
- **Forum:** Problems and solutions discussion

#### How to Use:
1. Search similar products in your category
2. Read their "About" and revenue milestones
3. Check what problems they solved
4. Look at their pricing and customer count
5. Read comments for challenges they faced

#### What You'll Find:
- Realistic revenue expectations
- Common challenges in your space
- Pricing strategies that work
- Time-to-market benchmarks
- Marketing channels that work

---

### 4. Product Hunt

**Best For:** Competitive analysis and launch patterns
**Reliability:** ★★★★☆ (High - real product data)
**Speed:** ★★★★☆ (Fast - good search)

#### What to Research:
1. Search for products in your category
2. Sort by: Most upvoted, Recent launches
3. Read top comments (what people love/hate)
4. Check "Similar Products" section
5. Note pricing and positioning

#### Key Metrics to Track:
- Upvotes (signals interest level)
- Comment themes (what resonates)
- Maker responses (how they positioned it)
- Follow-up products (market evolution)

#### Insights to Extract:
- What value props get attention
- Common complaints about existing solutions
- Pricing ranges that work
- Features that excite users
- Red flags that killed products

---

### 5. Google Trends

**Best For:** Market trends and search volume
**Reliability:** ★★★★★ (Excellent - real search data)
**Speed:** ★★★★★ (Very Fast)

#### How to Use:
1. Enter main keyword for your idea
2. Set timeframe: Past 5 years
3. Check if trend is rising, stable, or falling
4. Compare to competitor terms
5. Look at related queries

#### Analysis Framework:
- **Rising trend:** Growing market, good signal
- **Stable:** Mature market, harder to enter
- **Declining:** Dying market, avoid
- **Seasonal:** Plan for seasonality
- **Regional:** Check if global or local

#### Example Queries:
- "invoice software"
- "[your idea] vs [competitor]"
- Related problems and solutions

**Red Flags:**
- Declining search interest over 3+ years
- Very low absolute volume
- No related search queries
- Highly seasonal with short windows

**Best For:** Understanding competitor strengths/weaknesses
**Reliability:** ★★★★★ (Excellent - real user feedback)
**Speed:** ★★★☆☆ (Moderate - takes time to analyze)

#### Sites to Check:

**G2.com:**
- Detailed feature ratings
- Pro/con lists from users
- Comparison tools
- Verified reviews

**Capterra:**
- SMB focus
- Price comparisons
- User demographics
- Feature checklists

**Trustpilot:**
- Overall sentiment
- Recent reviews
- Response patterns
- Complaint trends

#### What to Extract:
- **1-star reviews:** What makes people angry?
- **5-star reviews:** What do users love?
- **3-star reviews:** What's "just okay"?
- **Feature requests:** What's missing?
- **Price complaints:** Too expensive? Too cheap?

#### Analysis Template:
```
Competitor: [Name]
Rating: X/5 (Y reviews)

Top Praised Features:
- [Feature 1]
- [Feature 2]

Top Complaints:
- [Complaint 1]
- [Complaint 2]

Missing Features:
- [Gap 1]
- [Gap 2]

Pricing Issues:
- [Issue]
```

## Research Workflow

### Quick Validation (30 minutes):
1. **5 min:** Google Trends - check market trend
2. **10 min:** Reddit - find pain points
3. **10 min:** Product Hunt - analyze competitors
4. **5 min:** Twitter - current discussions

### Deep Validation (2-3 hours):
1. **30 min:** Competitor website analysis (5-10 companies)
2. **30 min:** G2/Capterra reviews (read 50+ reviews)
3. **30 min:** Reddit deep-dive (read 100+ comments)
4. **30 min:** Indie Hackers research (similar products)
5. **30 min:** Document findings and score

### Expert Validation (1-2 days):
1. **Day 1 AM:** Deep research using all sources
2. **Day 1 PM:** Create customer interview questions
3. **Day 2 AM:** Conduct 5-10 customer interviews
4. **Day 2 PM:** Analyze findings and finalize report

## Research Red Flags

Watch for these warning signs:

### Demand Red Flags:
- ❌ Can't find anyone discussing this problem
- ❌ Existing discussions are 3+ years old
- ❌ Only entrepreneurs talking, not end users
- ❌ People say "nice idea" but no action
- ❌ No one willing to pay for solutions

### Competition Red Flags:
- ❌ 20+ very similar competitors
- ❌ Big tech companies already in space
- ❌ Competitors pivoting away from space
- ❌ Recent shutdowns in the category
- ❌ All competitors look identical

### Market Red Flags:
- ❌ Declining Google Trends over 2+ years
- ❌ Shrinking job market in target industry
- ❌ Regulatory issues emerging
- ❌ Technology obsolescence risk
- ❌ Extremely seasonal with short window

## Pro Tips

1. **Set time limits:** Research can be endless, cap it at 30-60 minutes
2. **Document sources:** Save links for future reference
3. **Look for patterns:** Single data points lie, patterns don't
4. **Check dates:** Old discussions might not reflect current market
5. **Verify claims:** Don't trust a single source
6. **Ask "why":** Dig deeper than surface complaints
7. **Follow money:** Look for evidence people pay for solutions
8. **Be skeptical:** Filter out noise and self-promotion

Remember: The goal of research is to find truth, not confirmation of your hopes.
